# Rest Periods

How long you rest between sets matters more than you might think.

## Why rest matters

Your muscles need time to recover between sets so you can lift the same weight again. Too little rest and your performance drops. Too much rest and your workout takes forever (and you cool down).

## General guidelines

| Goal | Rest between sets |
|------|------------------|
| **Strength** (heavy weight, 1-5 reps) | 3-5 minutes |
| **Muscle growth** (moderate weight, 6-12 reps) | 1-3 minutes |
| **Endurance** (light weight, 15+ reps) | 30-60 seconds |

**For beginners: rest 2-3 minutes between sets.** This is enough time to recover while keeping your workout moving. Don't overthink it.

## What to do while resting

- Catch your breath
- Take a sip of water
- Log your set in the app
- Stay near your equipment (don't wander off and lose your machine)

## Signs you need more rest

- You got 10 reps on set 1 but only 6 reps on set 2 with the same weight
- You feel dizzy or nauseous
- Your form is breaking down

If any of these happen, take an extra minute before your next set.

## Signs you're resting too long

- You feel completely fresh, like you didn't just do a set
- Your workout is taking over 90 minutes
- You're spending more time on your phone than lifting

## Rest between exercises

When you finish all sets of one exercise and move to the next, take 1-2 minutes. Use this time to adjust the machine or grab different weights.

## The bottom line

For beginners, 2-3 minutes between sets covers almost every situation. As you gain experience, you can fine-tune rest periods based on your goals. For now, don't stress about it -- just be consistent.
