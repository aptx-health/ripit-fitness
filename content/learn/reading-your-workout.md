# Reading Your Workout

When you open a workout, you'll see exercises with numbers and abbreviations. Here's what they all mean.

## Sets and reps

- **Set** = one group of consecutive reps. You rest between sets.
- **Rep** = one complete movement (lifting and lowering the weight once).

**"3x10"** means 3 sets of 10 reps. You do 10 reps, rest, 10 more reps, rest, 10 more reps. Done.

## RPE

Rate of Perceived Exertion, on a 1-10 scale. It measures how hard a set felt.

- **RPE 7** = You could have done 3 more reps
- **RPE 8** = You could have done 2 more reps
- **RPE 9** = You could have done 1 more rep
- **RPE 10** = You gave everything, no more reps possible

When a program says "3x10 @ RPE 8," it means: do 3 sets of 10, and each set should feel like you had about 2 reps left.

## RIR

Reps in Reserve -- the number of reps you could have done but didn't.

- **3 RIR** = same as RPE 7
- **2 RIR** = same as RPE 8
- **1 RIR** = same as RPE 9

RPE and RIR are two ways of saying the same thing.

## Weight notation

You might see weight written as:

- **135 lbs** -- a specific weight
- **BW** -- bodyweight (no added weight)
- **BW+25** -- bodyweight plus 25 lbs (for dips, pull-ups, etc.)

## What to log

After each set, record:
1. **Weight used**
2. **Reps completed** (you might not hit the target -- that's fine)
3. **RPE** (optional but helpful for tracking progress)

This data helps you decide when to increase weight next time.
