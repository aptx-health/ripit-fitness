# Pre-Workout Warm-Up

A good warm-up takes 5 minutes and makes your workout safer and more effective. Don't skip it.

## Step 1: Get your heart rate up (2-3 minutes)

Pick one and go at a moderate pace -- you should be slightly out of breath but able to talk.

- **Brisk walk** on the treadmill
- **Jumping jacks** (30 seconds on, 15 seconds rest, repeat)
- **Stationary bike** at an easy pace
- **Stair climber** at a slow speed

The goal is to warm up your muscles and get blood flowing. You don't need to break a heavy sweat.

## Step 2: Wake up your joints (1-2 minutes)

Move each joint through its full range of motion, slowly and gently. Do about 10 reps of each.

- **Arm circles** -- small circles, then big circles, forward and backward
- **Shoulder rolls** -- roll them forward 10 times, then backward 10 times
- **Hip circles** -- hands on hips, make big circles with your hips
- **Leg swings** -- hold onto something, swing one leg forward and back, then side to side
- **Neck rolls** -- slow, gentle circles (skip this if it causes discomfort)

## What NOT to do before lifting

**Don't do static stretching.** Holding a stretch for 30+ seconds before lifting can actually reduce your strength and power. Save static stretching for after your workout or on rest days.

Static stretching = holding a position (like touching your toes for 30 seconds).
Dynamic warm-up = moving through a range of motion (like arm circles). This is what you want.

## Warm-up sets

Before your first heavy exercise, do 1-2 warm-up sets with very light weight (about 50% of your working weight). This prepares the specific muscles you're about to use.

**Example:** If you're going to bench press 100 lbs, do:
- 10 reps at 50 lbs (warm-up set 1)
- 5 reps at 75 lbs (warm-up set 2)
- Then start your working sets at 100 lbs

## Cool-down (optional but helpful)

After your workout:

- **Walk for 3-5 minutes** to bring your heart rate down gradually
- **Static stretching** -- now is the right time. Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds:
  - Chest doorway stretch
  - Quad stretch (stand on one leg, pull foot to glute)
  - Hamstring stretch (foot on a bench, lean forward)
  - Shoulder cross-body stretch

Cooling down can reduce muscle soreness the next day, but it's not required. At minimum, don't go from heavy squats to sitting in your car immediately -- walk around for a few minutes.
