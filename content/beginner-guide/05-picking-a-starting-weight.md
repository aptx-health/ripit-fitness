# Picking a Starting Weight

Your first session is about finding the right weights, not setting records. Here's how.

## The two-set test

For each exercise:

1. **Set 1:** Pick the lightest weight on the machine (or a very light dumbbell). Do 10 reps. This is your warm-up -- it should feel easy.
2. **Set 2:** Go up 1-2 notches on the pin (or 5-10 lbs on dumbbells). Do 10 reps.

Now ask yourself:
- **Could I have done 15+ reps?** Go up another notch.
- **Could I have done about 3 more reps?** That's your working weight. Stay here.
- **Could I barely finish 10?** Go down a notch.

## Guidelines by exercise type

- **Large muscles (legs, back, chest):** You'll typically use more weight. Start with 30-50 lbs on machines.
- **Small muscles (biceps, triceps, shoulders):** Start with 10-20 lbs on machines or 5-10 lb dumbbells.

These are just starting points. Everyone is different.

## Write it down

Once you find a weight that feels right (RPE 7-8 for 10 reps), write it down. That's your starting point. Next session, use the same weight. If it felt too easy, go up one notch.

## Common mistakes

- **Starting too heavy:** Your first workout should feel almost too easy. There's always next time.
- **Comparing to others:** The person next to you has been training for years. Your weights are your weights.
- **Changing too fast:** Stick with a weight for at least 2 sessions before increasing. Your body needs time to learn the movement.

## When to go up in weight

Once you can complete all your prescribed sets and reps at RPE 7 or lower (it feels pretty easy), increase the weight by the smallest amount available -- usually one notch on a machine or 5 lbs on dumbbells.
