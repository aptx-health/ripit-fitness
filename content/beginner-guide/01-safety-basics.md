# Safety Basics

Your body gives you clear signals. Learn to listen to them.

## When to stop immediately

- **Lightheadedness or dizziness** -- Set the weight down, sit on a bench, and take slow breaths. This usually means you held your breath too long or pushed too hard.
- **Sharp or sudden joint pain** -- Pain in your knees, shoulders, or back that feels like a stab or pinch means something is wrong. Stop the exercise. Muscle soreness during a set is normal; sharp joint pain is not.
- **Numbness or tingling** -- If your hands, feet, or any limb goes tingly or numb, stop and rest.

## Breathing

Breathe out when you push or pull the weight (the hard part). Breathe in when you lower it (the easy part). Never hold your breath for an entire rep.

**Example:** On a chest press, breathe out as you push the handles away from you. Breathe in as you bring them back.

## Don't ego lift

Start lighter than you think you need to. Seriously. Nobody in the gym is watching how much weight you use, and using too much weight leads to bad form and injury. A lighter weight with good form builds more muscle than a heavy weight with sloppy form.

If you can't control the weight on the way down, it's too heavy.
