# How Gym Machines Work

Machines are beginner-friendly because they guide your movement. Here's what you need to know.

## Adjusting the seat

Most machines have a pin or lever on the side of the seat. Pull it, slide the seat to the right height, and let it click into place. The right height depends on the machine, but the general rule is: the handles or pads should line up with the joint you're moving.

- **Chest press / shoulder press:** Handles should be roughly at chest or shoulder height.
- **Leg press / leg curl:** Your knees should bend to about 90 degrees at the start.
- **Lat pulldown:** Adjust the thigh pad so it holds you snugly in the seat.

If something feels awkward, try one notch up or down. There's no single "correct" setting -- it depends on your body.

## Weight pins (weight stack machines)

Look for a stack of rectangular weight plates with holes in them. Pull the pin out, slide it into the hole next to the weight you want, and push it in until it clicks. The number on the plate is the weight in pounds (or kilograms -- check the label).

**Start light.** Pick a weight you can move easily for 10 reps. You can always add more.

## Cable attachments

Cable machines are versatile. They have a carabiner clip where you attach different handles:

- **Straight bar** -- for pulldowns, curls
- **Rope** -- for tricep pushdowns, face pulls
- **D-handle** -- for single-arm work

To swap attachments: squeeze the carabiner open, remove the old handle, clip on the new one.

## Plate-loaded machines

Some machines use round weight plates instead of a pin-stack. Slide plates onto the pegs and secure them with the clips provided. Always load both sides evenly.
