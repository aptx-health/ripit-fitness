# Range of Motion and Form

Good form means moving the weight through a full range of motion with control. That's it. No secrets.

## Full range of motion

Every exercise has a start position and an end position. Move through the complete distance between them.

- **Bicep curl:** Start with your arm fully extended. Curl all the way up until your hand is near your shoulder. Lower all the way back down.
- **Leg press:** Lower the platform until your knees are bent to about 90 degrees. Push it all the way back up (don't lock your knees).
- **Chest press:** Let the handles come back until you feel a stretch in your chest. Press all the way out.

Cutting the movement short (doing "half reps") means you're using more weight than you can actually handle. Drop the weight and do full reps instead.

## Control the movement

Every rep has two parts:

1. **The push/pull** (concentric) -- Move the weight with steady effort. About 1-2 seconds.
2. **The return** (eccentric) -- Lower the weight slowly and under control. About 2-3 seconds.

If the weight is crashing down or swinging wildly, it's too heavy. The lowering phase is where a lot of muscle growth happens, so don't rush it.

## No jerking or swinging

Momentum is the enemy of muscle growth. If you have to jerk your body or swing the weight to move it, reduce the weight. Every rep should look smooth and deliberate.

## A simple form checklist

Before each set, ask yourself:

- Am I moving through the full range of motion?
- Am I controlling the weight on the way down?
- Am I using the target muscle, not swinging my whole body?

If the answer is yes to all three, your form is good enough. Don't overthink it.
